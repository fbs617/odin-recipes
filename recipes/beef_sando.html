Japanese Beef Sando Recipe

Description

A Japanese beef sando, or Wagyu katsu sando, is a luxurious sandwich featuring a crispy, breaded beef cutlet encased in soft, pillowy Japanese milk bread. The beef, often high-quality Wagyu or another tender cut, is lightly seasoned, coated in flour, egg, and panko, then deep-fried to a perfect golden crisp while remaining juicy inside. The cutlet is then nestled between slices of toasted shokupan, spread with butter, Japanese mustard, and a rich, slightly tangy tonkatsu sauce that enhances the umami flavors.

The sandwich is traditionally served crustless, cut into neat halves or thirds, allowing the contrast of crispy beef and fluffy bread to shine. Its balance of textures and flavors—crunchy, juicy, buttery, and slightly sweet—makes it a simple yet indulgent treat. Best enjoyed fresh, the beef sando is a staple in high-end Japanese cafés and a favorite among those who appreciate the delicate balance of high-quality ingredients prepared with precision.

Ingredients

  • Wagyu beef (or filet mignon)
  • Salt and black pepper
  • All-purpose flour
  • Egg
  • Panko Breadcrumbs
  • Neutral oil (for frying)
  • Japanese milk bread (shokupan)
  • Butter
  • Tonkatsu sauce
  • Japanese mustard (karashi) or Dijon mustard (optional)

Steps

  1. Season the beef with salt and pepper, then coat it in flour, beaten egg, and panko breadcrumbs.
  2. Fry in hot oil (350°F/175°C) for about 45-60 seconds per side until golden and crispy.
  3. Drain on a wire rack and let rest for a few minutes.
  4. Lightly toast the milk bread and spread with butter, mustard (if using), and tonkatsu sauce.
  5. Place the beef cutlet between the bread, press gently, trim the crusts, and slice into halves or thirds.
  6. Serve immediately and enjoy!
